About Me

I am currently pending graduation from Duquesne University with my M.S. in Biological Sciences. My research interests include molecular biology, computational biology, and genetics. My research experience encompasses a wide variety of topics from amphibian ecology to phospholipid metabolism in yeast. I graduated from the University of Pittsburgh in 2024 with my B.S. in Biological Sciences. During my time at Pitt, I worked under the mentorship of Dr. Cori Richards-Zawacki studying amphibian ecology and infectious disease. As part of an undergraduate research program, I successfully isolated 4 strains of the amphibian chytrid fungus Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis from the Pymatuning Reservoir. Following in my senior year I performed various experiments with my isolates, including determination of optimal growth conditions and bacterial metabolite challenge assays. My research experience with Dr. Richards-Zawacki greatly inspired me to pursue research in biology as a profession. I continued to graduate education at Duquesne University, where I will soon recieve my M.S. in Biological Sciences. My first rotation in the program examined protein-protein interactions of the fungal-like bacteria Streptomyces venezuelae. My second rotation and continued Masters research utilized the model organism, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, to study phospholipid metabolism and molecular responses to common lipid metabolites. I learned valuable skills such as plasmid construction, bacterial 2-hybrid, qRT-PCR, radiolabeing, WGS, and much more. My continuation of research during my Masters has solidified my desire to pursue a professional career in biology. I am enthusiatically seeking out research and professional opportunities to continue down this career path.
