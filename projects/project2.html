Masters Research (2)

Abstract: The fungal-like bacteria Streptomyces venezuelae is a useful organism in the study of bacterial cell biology as it contains many genes contributing to cell division, both characterized and uncharacterized. During my first year rotations, I investigated protein-protein interactions to assist a resident PhD student in her dissertation research. Using bacterial 2-hybrid assays, we characterized multiple interaction domains on the dynamin-like protein DynB and the uncharacterized protein SsdA. Both proteins play an important role in septation and chromosome segregation during cell division.

Methods

This project used a combination of plasmid construction, PCR, transformation, and bacterial two-hybrid assays.

Visuals

Images or figures can be included here if permissions allow.

Results & Key Takeaways

After construction of multiple plasmids expressing specific segments of the DynB protein, the bacterial 2-hybrid assay successfully identified which regions are required for interaction with SsdA. The results of this rotation project will be used to assist the resident PhD student with her dissertation work, ultimately furthering our understanding of the functionality and structure of SsdA in S. venezuelae.
